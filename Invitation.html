Why This Works Together

You've already told us what you're looking for: a real break for soul and mind. That's not something one pillar can give on its own.

A calmer body allows a quieter mind. A quieter mind directs the body with more intention. What you eat sustains both. And without a clear next step, even the deepest reset fades within days of going home. Ayurvana's three days are sequenced so each part carries into the next, rather than existing as separate sessions on a schedule.

This is exactly why three days in Ubud works differently than fitting a class in between meetings. The shift happens because everything works on you at once, not in isolation.

Settle the body. Quiet the mind. Restore the system. Decide what's next.

We see this as one continuous process — a returning to the self before returning to the noise.

How Dates Work

We run Ayurvana in small, unhurried cohorts across June, July and August — never more than 12 guests at a time, so each group gets our full attention. That's why we don't publish a single fixed date months in advance. Instead, we confirm each cohort as it comes together, and give you at least three weeks' notice to plan your travel around it.

If you have a preferred window within the season, let us know when you get in touch — we'll always try to place you in the cohort that suits you best.
